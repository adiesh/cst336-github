Machine learning can be applied to wide variety of events, from elections to sporting events.

If any of you saw Back to the Future Part II movie, the antagonist Biff Tannen had a book called "Grays Sports Almanac" which had the outcome of every sporting event from 1950 to the year 2000.

Biff became very wealthy since he could bet on every sporting event within that timeframe.



The closest thing to the "Grays Sports Almanac" would be using Machine Learning that can predict these contests.

Humans tend to have a bias for certain teams or players due to previous accomplishments.

Through machine learning, we can make better recruitment to assess the best options.



The Method:

The first stage of putting together a model would be to gather data from previous contests.

This means looking at each team's previous records over the last couple years. Keep in mind regarding the following:

Factors: Reasons:
Margin of the Victory How many points did that particular team win or lose by?
Points scored per quarter Some teams score more or less throughout the game. They gain a significant lead from the start, or had to comeback to win games.
Player's stats A player's rushing, passing, or receiving yards. How well did he perform against certain teams?
Player's injury history Injuries or surgical procedures a player went through. How many games were or will be missed due to injury?
Team's lineup Lineups are bound to change due to injury or suspensions.
Divisional or Non-Conference Game Divisional contests are much more competitive since your record heavily depends on wins within the division, and each team meets twice. Non-conference games tend to be not as competitive as divisional contests.
Location of Contest Home or away game? Home has the advantage. Air levels can vary. Denver is 1 mile above sea-level, while some games occur in Mexico City in recent years (7,200 feet above sea-level)
Attendance Did the stadium have full attendance? Certain stadium's acoustics are very loud, making it difficult to make proper calls
Weather conditions Wind and snow makes it difficult to make field goals. Rain makes it difficult to throw the ball.

There are other factors you can keep in mind, but all of these could help play a role in the outcome of a specific contest.

The second stage of putting a model together would be analyzing social media.

Social Media used were:

  • Twitter
  • Facebook
  • YouTube

Going through these social media websites can help predict outcomes with more accuracy.

The model picks up on what social media users are saying regarding:

  • Injuries
  • Suspensions
  • Changes to a lineup
  • Controversies
  • Weather conditions in a given region.
